# Benchmark Patterns

Use this when improving the skill against strong business, B2B, corporate, professional service, and proposal-grade websites.

## What Strong Business Websites Consistently Do

- Explain value fast: first viewport names the audience, problem, offer, proof, and next action.
- Reduce buyer risk: show credentials, customers, case evidence, security/compliance, process, and current content.
- Support long consideration cycles: provide scannable summaries, detail pages, downloadable proof, comparison content, and clear internal navigation.
- Convert without pressure: one primary CTA, secondary research CTA, useful contact path, and no fake urgency.
- Make complex offers understandable: use diagrams, workflows, calculators, industry maps, or before/after comparisons.
- Preserve credibility: clean navigation, professional visual quality, specific copy, current facts, and no unsupported superlatives.

## Patterns To Add When The Source Supports Them

- Buyer-role paths: CEO/CFO/technical/operations views for complex B2B.
- Industry scenario matrix: industries by pain point, solution, proof, and CTA.
- Proof library: case studies, certifications, partner logos, reports, patents, media, or downloadable PDFs.
- ROI/diagnosis module: only when formulas or assumptions are transparent.
- Service package comparison: for agencies and professional services.
- Resource center: reports, insights, FAQ, procurement support, or implementation guides.
- Sticky conversion rail: for long pages, keep contact/demo/consultation one click away.
- Trust footer: legal, privacy, ICP/permit info, contact, social proof, and sitemap.

## Mature Skill Behaviors To Emulate

- Require a short intake before major implementation.
- Produce an evidence map before writing factual claims.
- Offer 2 to 3 design directions instead of assuming a single style.
- Create a sitemap/section outline before writing code.
- Define exit criteria for every phase.
- Run deterministic checks and report evidence, not vibes.
- Keep references modular so the agent loads only what it needs.

## Avoid

- Treating business websites as generic landing pages.
- Over-optimizing for animation while neglecting proof and conversion.
- Copying SaaS patterns into industrial or regulated sectors without adapting trust signals.
- Adding ecommerce checkout assumptions to non-ecommerce business sites.
- Creating fake testimonials, fake customer logos, fake news, or fake form submissions.
